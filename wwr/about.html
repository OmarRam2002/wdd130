History

Whitewater rafting has transformed from a vital survival skill to a world-class adventure sport over the past two centuries. While indigenous peoples navigated wild rivers for thousands of years using wooden vessels, the modern era began in 1840 when Lieutenant John Fremont designed the first rubber raft to survey the Rocky Mountains. Commercial rafting truly took root in 1940 with guided trips on

the Snake and Salmon Rivers, fueled further by the post-WWII availability of military surplus rafts. The industry surged into the mainstream during the 1960s and 1970s, gaining international fame with its debut at the 1972 Munich Olympics. Today, fueled by technological leaps like self-bailing floors, rafting is a global phenomenon that combines high-adrenaline thrills with a deep commitment to river conservation.
